You're looking at it.

Built on [Astro Nano](https://github.com/markhorn-dev/astro-nano) as a starting point, then customized heavily until it stopped looking like a template and started looking like me.

## What's in here

- **Blog** — long-form writing, mostly about ideas I can't stop thinking about
- **Musings** — shorter thoughts, observations, things I'd otherwise text to one person
- **Art** — photography, watercolor, sketches, visual experiments
- **Projects** — things I've built, shipped, or at least gotten to a state worth showing

## Stack

- [Astro](https://astro.build) for static site generation
- [Tailwind CSS](https://tailwindcss.com) for styling
- [Vercel](https://vercel.com) for deployment
- Custom color palette, hand-drawn logo, and a possibly excessive number of hexagons

## Why build your own?

Because every time I tried to use someone else's blog platform I spent more time configuring it than writing. At least when I'm fiddling with padding values here, I'm fiddling with *my* padding values.

Also I wanted a bouncing rocket button. No existing platform was going to give me that. ⬢
